Dot-size consistency — three options

Each card shows a ~200-dot cluster (left) and a stacked timeline column representing a typical busy year ≈200 incidents (right). Colours are sampled from the harm-bucket palette in stack order. Timeline radius is fixed at 2.5 across all three configs because that's the only value where 2024's 404 dots fit; the cluster radius is the variable.

What you're seeing

  • Current. Cluster dots are 8 px radius. The cluster takes ~250 px across — feels like a substantial cloud. Transition to timeline drops to 2.5 px — a 3.2× shrink.
  • Option A. Everything at 2.5. Cluster shrinks to ~90 px across — looks like a small dot fog. Transition is seamless because nothing changes size.
  • Option B. Cluster dots at 5. Cluster ≈160 px across — still reads as a distinct cloud, but the shrink to timeline is 2× instead of 3.2×.

Why the timeline can't be bigger

2024 has 404 incidents. At radius 5 / pitch 11, a year column fits only ~84 dots — about 1/5 of what 2024 needs. The 2.5 radius / 6 pitch is the largest value that satisfies the capacity constraint with headroom. So the consistency question is really "how should the cluster scale toward 2.5", not "what radius everywhere."

The honest tradeoff

  • Cluster impact vs. transition smoothness are opposed. Bigger cluster dots = more visual gravity in Scene 1 = bigger jump to Scene 2.
  • Option B is the sweet spot if you want the cluster to still feel like the "wow, how many" moment without the radius animation feeling like a hard cut.
  • Option A is right if you decide Scene 1's job isn't "wow how many" but "here's the dataset" — i.e., the cluster is a transitional state, not a set-piece.
